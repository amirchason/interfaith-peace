## üìä RESEARCH SYNTHESIS: WHAT WORKS IN COUNTER-EXTREMISM

### Key Findings from Global Deradicalization Programs:

**1. SAUDI ARABIA'S PREVENTION, REHABILITATION, AND AFTER-CARE (PRAC) PROGRAM**
- **Success Rate**: 80-90% no reoffending
- **Methods**:
  - Theological re-education by moderate imams
  - Psychological counseling
  - Job training and economic support
  - Family involvement (critical)
  - Aftercare for 1-2 years post-release
- **Key Insight**: **Extremism has religious, psychological, AND socioeconomic roots‚Äîmust address all three**

**2. DENMARK'S AARHUS MODEL**
- **Approach**: "Hug a terrorist" (reintegration, not punishment)
- **Methods**:
  - No arrests for returning foreign fighters (unless committed crimes)
  - Mentorship programs
  - Education and employment support
  - Community reintegration
- **Results**: ~50% successfully reintegrated
- **Key Insight**: **Social inclusion is more effective than marginalization in preventing reradicalization**

**3. SINGAPORE'S RELIGIOUS REHABILITATION GROUP (RRG)**
- **Focus**: Theological counter-narratives
- **Methods**:
  - Moderate Muslim scholars engage detainees
  - Quran and Hadith study showing extremist misinterpretations
  - Family counseling
  - Community support post-release
- **Key Insight**: **Extremists often have shallow religious knowledge‚Äîdepth reduces extremism**

**4. MARRAKESH DECLARATION (2016) - Islamic Scholars**
- **260+ Muslim scholars from 120 countries** declared:
  - ISIS/Al-Qaeda pervert Islam
  - Killing innocents is **haram** (forbidden)
  - Religious minorities in Muslim lands have full citizenship rights
  - Jihad has strict conditions; terrorism is not jihad
- **Key Insight**: **Moderate Muslim majority exists; must be amplified**

**5. LIFE AFTER HATE (USA) - Former White Supremacists**
- **Founded by**: Ex-neo-Nazis
- **Methods**:
  - Peer-to-peer counseling
  - Addressing trauma that led to radicalization
  - Building empathy through interfaith dialogue
  - Vocational support
- **Key Insight**: **Former extremists are most credible voices for prevention**

## üß† ROOT CAUSES OF RELIGIOUS EXTREMISM (EVIDENCE-BASED)

### 1. **POLITICAL FACTORS**
- Foreign occupation/military intervention
- Authoritarianism (no democratic outlets)
- Grievances (real or perceived injustice)
- State violence against religious groups

### 2. **ECONOMIC FACTORS**
- Poverty (though not all poor become extremists)
- Unemployment (especially educated youth)
- Economic humiliation
- Lack of opportunity

### 3. **PSYCHOLOGICAL FACTORS**
- Identity crisis (especially diaspora youth)
- Search for meaning/purpose
- Trauma (war, abuse, loss)
- Need for belonging/community
- Personality traits (need for closure, desire for significance)

### 4. **THEOLOGICAL/IDEOLOGICAL FACTORS**
- **Simplistic, literalist interpretation** of scripture
- **Us vs. them mentality** (dualism)
- **Apocalypticism** (end-times urgency)
- **Selective reading** (ignoring peace verses, emphasizing conflict verses)
- **Charismatic leaders** misinterpreting texts

### 5. **SOCIAL FACTORS**
- Social media echo chambers
- Peer pressure
- Family radicalization
- Marginalization from mainstream society
